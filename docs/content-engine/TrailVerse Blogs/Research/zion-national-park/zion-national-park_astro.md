## Light pollution geometry

- **Springdale glow** — warm light dome to the **south** from park entrance corridor
- **Canyon floor** — walls block horizon early; can help or hurt compositions
- **Cleanest directions:** generally **south** toward open plateau; avoid pointing into Springdale for core shots

## Milky Way window (latitude ~37°N)

- Core visible roughly **March–October**; peak **June–August**
- Core rises **southeast**, transits **south** at modest altitude (~23° at Canyonlands latitude reference; similar for Zion)
- **Astronomical darkness** ~90–105 min after sunset; summer ~10:00–10:30 p.m.; May/Sept ~9:30 p.m.

## Best shooting locations (3)

### 1. Canyon Overlook / east rim pullouts
- Open horizon pockets above canyon; dramatic sandstone silhouettes
- Access: paved trail by day; at night park road pullouts — check current NPS night access rules
- Springdale dome to south — compose away from town when possible

### 2. Kolob Canyons section
- Bortle closer to **3**; less Springdale scatter
- Fewer shuttle crowds; longer drive from Springdale (~40+ min)
- Foreground: high plateau + distant peaks

### 3. Human History Museum / Pa'rus Trail corridor (ranger program areas)
- NPS hosts **stargazing programs** seasonally — good for scouting safe parking
- Lower elevation than rim; more light scatter — use for education nights + test shots
