## What makes Zion genuinely unique

- **Vertical Navajo sandstone canyon** carved by the Virgin River — depth and walls, not just red rock vistas
- **Shuttle-controlled main canyon** for much of the year (private vehicles banned on Zion Canyon Scenic Drive during shuttle season)
- **Angels Landing** chains section — permit required 24/7 for Scout Lookout → summit
- **The Narrows** — in-river hiking with flash-flood closure protocol (not a traditional trail)
- **International Dark Sky Park** (completed Utah “Mighty Five” dark sky set) — night programs are part of park identity
- **Kolob Canyons** northwest unit — different geology, less canyon-shuttle chaos

## Visitor stats & crowd patterns

- **~4.9M recreation visits in 2025** (preliminary); among busiest U.S. national parks
- Visitation up ~90% vs 2010 while staffing down — pressure on shuttles, trails, SAR
- **Peak months:** June historically ~12%+ of annual visits; January ~2.4% (winter drive-your-own-canyon secret)
- **Shuttle boardings:** ~5.2M (2022); fleet of 26 electric buses (2024)
- **2025 SAR:** 376 EMS responses; rangers report heavy one-on-one contacts on shuttles/trails

## Trail difficulty — editorial (not a trail list)

| Trail / experience | Reality |
|--------------------|---------|
| **Angels Landing** | Exposure + chains; permit lottery; rangers check permits anywhere on route; not for height anxiety |
| **The Narrows (bottom-up)** | No wilderness permit for day hike from Temple of Sinawava; **always in water**; cold; flash-flood kills — check flow + forecast morning-of |
| **Riverside Walk** | Best easy corridor win; paved; gateway to Narrows wade |
| **Canyon Overlook** | Short, high payoff; often skipped |
| **Observation Point** | Epic views when open — check status (trail damage/closure cycles) |
| **Emerald Pools** | Family-friendly until heat/crowds peak |
| **Kolob Canyons** | Quieter; different trip shape |

**Rescues / mistakes:** Narrows hypothermia; underestimating shuttle last-run (up to 8-mile walk if you miss last bus); Angels without permit ($5k fine / jail per NPS enforcement messaging).

## Wildlife & safety

- Desert bighorn sheep, mule deer, ringtails; **do not approach wildlife**
- **Flash floods** in narrow canyons — number-one Narrows risk
- **Heat** in summer canyon floor; cold water in Narrows even in July
- **Cliff edges** on rim trails — stay on constructed paths

## Seasonal strategy

| Season | What changes |
|--------|----------------|
| **Spring** | Shuttle starts ~Mar 7; wildflowers; Narrows cold; crowds build |
| **Summer** | Peak shuttle season; long days; afternoon storms; brutal lines |
| **Fall** | Best balance for many hikers; lottery competition |
| **Winter** | Shuttle off ~Dec–early Mar window — **you can drive Zion Canyon Scenic Drive**; fewer people; ice in shade |

## Gateway & logistics (no fees/hours)

- **Springdale, UT** — walkable park entrance, gear rental, lodging, Springdale Line shuttle
- **Round-trip shuttle timing:** ~90 min if you ride through; last buses critical — missing last run = long walk
- **Angels permits:** Recreation.gov seasonal + day-before lottery (12:01 a.m.–3 p.m. MT day prior); **not same-day** at visitor center
- **Top-down Narrows** — wilderness permit required (different trip)
