Quick heads up before I plan: I need two things to nail this for you —

**Who's coming?** (solo, couple, kids?) and **roughly when?** Timing matters a lot here — bison with calves in spring vs. rut in fall are completely different vibes.

That said, here's a solid 2-day framework based on what I know:

**Day 1 — Wildlife Loop + Bison Center**
Hit the 18-mile Wildlife Loop Road early — like 6:30am early. Bison are most active at dawn, and you'll have the road to yourself. Budget 2-3 hours for the loop itself. Lodging: custerresorts.com has cabins and lodges inside the park — book early, they fill up. Camping at Legion Lake or Center Lake if you'd rather be under the stars.

Go with Zion — it's the more immersive first-timer experience, and October is genuinely one of its best months.

The fall colors along the Virgin River are peak, crowds drop noticeably from summer, and the weather is ideal — highs around the mid-70s, cool mornings, low humidity. The shuttle system means you don't need to stress about parking. For first-timers, the canyon floor hikes alone (Riverside Walk, Canyon Overlook) are stunning without needing to attempt anything strenuous. One heads up: **The Narrows is closed right now due to toxic cyanobacteria in the Virgin River** — that's a big one to know before you go. Angels Landing requires a permit (grab it at [Recreation.gov](https://www.recreation.gov/permits/4675324) for summer/fall dates). No timed-entry confirmation for 2026, so expect packed trailheads — arrive before 7am for popular spots.

Bryce is worth a day-trip add-on if you have 4+ days. The hoodoos are unlike anything else, and the Navajo Loop (2.9mi, 550ft gain, ~1.5hr, Moderate) is a perfect intro. But as a standalone first-timer destination, Zion wins on variety and wow factor. Bryce becomes the better pick if you're into stargazing or photography — it's darker skies and the hoodoos at sunrise are otherworldly.

If you're doing both, do Zion first, Bryce second. Classic Utah combo and totally doable in 5-6 days from Las Vegas.

One thing to flag though: there's active toxic cyanobacteria in the Virgin River right now. It's not a closure, but you should avoid swallowing the water and wash off thoroughly after. Keep kids and dogs out of the water entirely.
